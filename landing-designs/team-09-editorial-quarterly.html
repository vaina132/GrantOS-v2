I.
Capabilities

The tools, plainly stated.

A field inventory Assembled by the
GrantLume editors
Section 01

A proposal calendar that keeps its own counsel.

All deadlines, call windows and internal review dates in one dated list. Framework-programme rhythms and national calls arrive pre-stamped, with sensible reminders that never shout.

Section 02

A budget ledger that reads like prose.

Budgets as plain accounts: personnel, equipment, overhead. Multi-year columns, currency conversion at the footer, and a clean PDF export that a finance officer can actually sign.

Section 03

A compliance folder that keeps itself.

Ethics approvals, data-management plans, GDPR artefacts and signed consortium agreements live together, versioned quietly. An auditor walks in; you hand over a folder, already tidy.

Section 04

Collaborators, kept in the loop without noise.

PIs, co-Is, admin and finance each get a role-appropriate view. Comments read like margin notes rather than a chat feed; @-mentions arrive once, by email, and wait.

Section 05

Reporting, in language a trustee will read.

Interim and final reports generated from your live data with the archive's tone — tables where tables belong, sentences where sentences do. Branded templates for each funder, legible at A4.

II.
Method

On a calmer submission week.

In four movements A photo-essay
without photographs
i.

Open the issue

Sign in and import your open calls, partners and recurring budget lines. Takes a morning; most admins do it over coffee.

ii.

Lay out the spread

Draft proposals from templates, invite co-Is by email, assemble a budget in readable columns. The cursor feels unhurried.

iii.

Send to press

Submit to the funder portal with a single checked export, and keep the signed copy, dated, in the compliance folder.

iv.

Archive the issue

Post-award, the reporting calendar takes over — milestones, spend, narrative sections — and the project moves, quietly, into the archive.
